This site is made using the HTML editing application Brackets and the topic is inspired by a research paper I wrote earlier this year on Video games and Longevity. The sources listed below are the same ones used in the paper.

Works Cited

  • Call of Duty 4 Modern Warfare Player Count. Steam Charts, 30 June 2012, steamcharts.com/app/7940. Accessed 23 Sept. 2019. Chart. Player count of a game that came out in the same year on the same platform as Team Fortress 2.
  • Choudhury, Saheli Roy, et al. "CEO of video game maker Nexon flags the 'single most important idea' in his industry." CNBC, 19 Mar. 2018, www.cnbc.com/2018/03/19/video-games-nexon-ceo-says-longevity-is-critical-for-video-games.html. Accessed 22 Sept. 2019. This article is directly from an interview with a game director and provides an opinion on the importance of game longevity and how it should be handled.
  • History.com Editors. "Video Game History." HISTORY, A&E Television Networks, 1 Sept. 2017, www.history.com/topics/inventions/history-of-video-gamess. Accessed 22 Sept. 2019. History.com is a recognized and well-edited online source for most topics history-related. This article provides a history of video games that could provide context for my claims.
  • Lahti, Evan. "How Team Fortress 2 changed FPSes forever." PC Gamer, 24 May 2018, www.pcgamer.com/how-team-fortress-2-changed-fpses-forever/. Accessed 22 Sept. 2019. PC Gamer is a well-known gaming webpage that provides stories in all varieties of video games. This particular article focuses in on my case study, Team Fortress Two, and one of the things that sets it apart from other video games in its genre.
  • New York Film Academy. "Three Creative Ways Developers Are Increasing The Lifespan Of Games." New York Film Academy Student Resources, 26 Sept. 2014, www.nyfa.edu/student-resources/developers-increasing-lifespan-games/. Accessed 22 Sept. 2019. As the title suggests, this article provides examples of how game developers increase the lifespan of a game that can be analyzed. The NYFA is a reputable source and has a site full of readings for students.
  • Stuart, Keith. "Games Gamer communities: the positive side." Games blog, The Guardian, 31 July 2013, www.theguardian.com/technology/gamesblog/2013/jul/31/gamer-communities-positive-side-twitter. Accessed 22 Sept. 2019. This source provides insight into the communities and the positive effects that the bond of gaming has between people.
  • Team Fortress 2 player count from June 2012 to September 2019. Steam Charts, 30 June 2012, steamcharts.com/app/440#1y. Accessed 22 Sept. 2019. Chart. Provides a player count for TF2 that can be used as an evidence of its still active community.
  • "TF2 is a Timeless Masterpiece." Youtube, uploaded by Lazy Purple, 18 Nov. 2017, www.youtube.com/watch?v=KuqImZKygvw. Accessed 22 Sept. 2019. LazyPurple is a respected player in the Team Fortress 2 community that has a history of making videos about the game and goes into depth on some of the intricacies that made TF2 last for so long.
